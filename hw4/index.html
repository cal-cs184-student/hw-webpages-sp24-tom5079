HW 4: Clothsim

Overview

In Clothsim, we constructed a cloth model as a system of masses and springs to simulate physical interactions, used integration to compute total force and mass positions, and wrote shaders to apply textures to our objects. We experimented with different constraints and properties, comparing how they affect the cloth’s properties and behavior throughout the simulation. We applied different shaders to achieve varying types of materials.

Part 5: Shaders

A shader refers to small programs that run in parallel on a graphical processing unit (GPU) to execute small parts of the graphics pipeline. They are used to accelerate rendering operations, being especially useful for ray tracing and real-time/interactive applications. They generally take input in the form of attributes and uniforms, and output a single 4-dimensional vector, which may represent position or color of a vertex or fragment (a pixel). A shader program is created by linking a vertex shader to a fragment shader, which takes a vertex input and outputs the fragment color.

Blinn-Phong (Specular) shading combines ambient, diffuse, and specular shadings to give a material a realistic look without any ray tracing. It computes the components, multiplies each by a coefficient, and sums them together. The resulting material looks reflective and slightly glossy.

Bump mapping uses the height map to modify the normal vectors of an object such that its texture appears spatially detailed (bumpy) when it is applied while preserving the original geometry. Displacement mapping modifies the actual positions of vertices to reflect the height map, changing the object’s geometry. Bump mapping is usually a satisfactory alternative to displacement mapping and requires notably less computing power as it does not modify the geometry. However there are cases where the additional detail provided by displacement mapping may be desired. In the above screenshots, bump mapping “darkens” some areas of the textures to give the illusion of depth, whereas displacement mapping results in real bumps.

The previous screenshots set the resolution of the sphere to 16x16. Raising the resolution by increasing -o and -a to 128 allows the displacement mapped sphere to more accurately and finely reflect the height map. The bump mapped sphere showed little difference.
